Logistics

Time

We're morning people. We're having a late morning ceremony, followed by a lunch reception.

The ceremony will commence at 11 am. Feel free to arrive at the Grad House any time after 10:30 am.

The reception will take place directly after the ceremony, and will finish at 3 pm.

Venue

We met at the University of Waterloo, and we're getting married at the University of Waterloo!

While the U of W is home to over 28,000 students, February 17 falls on reading week, so campus will be quite sparse. Though, you may see some of the U of W's 3000 grad students, who may not realize it's reading week.

Both the ceremony and reception will take place at the Grad House, on the south side of campus.

Parking

There are two options for parking.

The closest lot to the Grad House is lot HV (circled in the picture to the left. It is a paid lot; the cost is $2 per hour up to a maximum of $10.

The University Plaza, to the east of Ring Road, also has ample parking at no charge. Note, however, that this parking is intended for Plaza customers only, so you may get a ticket if you head straight to campus.

Directions

If you're coming from lot HV, the most straightforward route is to cross Ring Road and go up the stairs to South Campus Hall (SCH). Enter and go straight through the building. When you exit, continue straight and the Grad House (GH) will be on your left.

If you're coming from the Plaza, cross Ring Road, and take the path between Engineering 2 (E2) and Douglas Wright Engineering (DWE). When you reach a set of stairs, turn left and you should see the Grad House (GH). This path is entirely outside, so if you enter a building, you've gone astray!

If you're coming by public transit, get off near University and Seagram, and follow the directions for lot HV.

Food

We're pleased to have the Grad House catering. Coffee, tea, hot chocolate and wine (white and red) will be available. For other beverages, the Grad House has a well-stocked bar and several local beers on tap.

For dessert, we will be serving some cupcakes that we baked. The Irvine and Bekolay families have both published a cute cookbook, so we took a vanilla and chocolate cupcake recipe from these cookbooks to be served at the wedding. The recipes can be found below.

Gramma Bekolay's Chocolate Cake

  • 2 eggs
  • 2-2/3 cups flour
  • 2/3 cup cocoa
  • 2 cups boiling water
  • 2/3 cup margarine
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 tsp. vanilla
  • 2 tsp. baking powder
  • 1 tsp. baking soda
  • 1 pinch salt

Heat oven to 350 F. Mix margarine and sugar until fluffly. Add eggs. Beat 3 - 4 minutes. Add vanilla. Boil and mix 1 cup of water with the cocoa and stir. Add to sugar mixture and mix slowly so as not to splash. Mix dry ingredients together then add alternately with remaining cup of water. Mix until smooth. Bake in a glass pan for 45 minutes at 325 F.

Gran Irvine's Chocolate Icing

  • 1/2 cup crisco (not oil)
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup evaporated milk
  • 3 cup icing sugar
  • 1 tsp. vanilla

Mix Crisco, cocoa, and evaporated milk in mixer. Add icing sugar until desired consistency. Add vanilla.

Aunt Diane's Rainbow Dot Cake

  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 cup margarine
  • 2 cups flour
  • 1/2 tsp. salt
  • 3 tsp. baking soda
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 1/2 tsp. vanilla
  • Rainbow dots
  • 3 egg whites
  • 1/4 icing sugar

Cream margarine, add sugar. Mix in flour, salt and baking powder alternately with milk. Add in order 1 1/2 tsp. vanilla, rainbow dots. Fold in 3 egg whites beaten with 1/4 cup white sugar. Bake at 350 for 1/2 hour.

Aunt Caroline's Royal Icing

  • 4 cups icing sugar
  • 2/3 cups butter
  • 1/3 cups Crisco
  • 0.5 egg white
  • 1/4 tsp. vanilla

Mix the egg white with enough water to make 1/4 cup. Blend all ingredients. Thicken with more sugar if necessary.
